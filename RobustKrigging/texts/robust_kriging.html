There is a formula for calculating estimate predicted value. The formula uses values of nearby points and their weights.

Robust statistic very often uses lognormal kriging. It is a kind of kriging, which is logarithmicly transformed to testing data. At first the data are trasformed to natural logarithm, eventually into decadic logarithm. The result is in the end reversed. It is necessary to fix it by systematic errors.

Lognormal kriging: $$Y(x) = \log (Z(x))$$

Reverse including the correction of systematic error: $$Z(x_0) = \exp(Y(x_0)+\frac{\sigma_{Y}^2}{2})$$

The technique of lognormal kriging is very often used because of it decreasing scatter of testing data. The result is still partly distorted, because the data is dependent on semivariogram.
